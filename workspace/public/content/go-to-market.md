# Care Exchange — Go-to-Market Strategy

---

## Product Overview

Care Exchange is a B2B2C SaaS platform connecting NDIS participants, Support Coordinators, and Service Providers through consent-driven care record sharing.

**Core value proposition:** Participants control a single, shareable care record. Providers and coordinators connect via secure referral links. Progress updates are structured, not PDF reports.

**Target users:** NDIS Plan-managed and self-managed participants (not agency-managed), Support Coordinators, Allied Health providers, Plan Managers.

---

## Market Opportunity

### NDIS Landscape
- **~$35B annual NDIS budget** (2024-25)
- **~640,000 participants** with active plans
- **~20,000 registered service providers**
- **5-7% annual growth** in participant numbers
- **Support coordination budget:** ~$1.2B/year
- **Average Support Coordinator manages 25-40 participants**

### Pain Points Driving Demand
- **Fragmentation:** Average participant has 4-6 providers, none connected
- **Documentation burden:** Therapists spend 20-30% of time on admin
- **Information loss:** Provider transitions cause weeks of re-assessment
- **NDIS Commission compliance:** Requires audit-ready records
- **Plan underspend:** ~$2B in unspent NDIS funds annually

### Market Size
- **SAM:** Plan-managed and self-managed participants (~45% of total) = ~288,000 participants
- **TAM:** Full NDIS ecosystem = ~640,000 participants
- **ARR potential at $30/participant/month:** $103M annually

## Target Segments & Prioritization

### Phase 1: Support Coordinators (Entry Point)
**Why first:** Small number, high leverage. One coordinator touches 25-40 participants.

- High pain from fragmentation (managing 4-6 providers per participant)
- Budget to purchase (Support Coordination is a funded line item)
- Advocates for platform to providers and participants
- Champions adoption from within

**Target:** 50 Support Coordinators in first 6 months
**Acquisition cost target:** <$500/coordinator
**Churn risk:** Low — high switching cost once caseload is onboarded

### Phase 2: Allied Health Providers (Expansion)
**Why second:** Providers are brought in by coordinators and participants.

- Reduce intake time (currently 2-4 weeks)
- Meet NDIS Commission documentation standards
- Compete on care quality, not just availability
- Cross-provider collaboration without extra meetings

**Target:** 200 allied health therapists across 30 clinics
**Acquisition:** Partner with 2-3 major allied health groups first

### Phase 3: Plan Managers (Deepen Moat)
**Why third:** Financial visibility makes everything stickier.

- Verify services against invoices automatically
- Identify underspend before plans expire
- Compliance and audit trail for NDIS Commission
- Gateway to deeper financial product features

### Phase 4: Participants & Families (Direct)
**Why last:** B2C acquisition is expensive; coordinators bring them in.

- Direct app for participants with self-managed plans
- Family member access features
- Public-facing provider directory

## Pricing Strategy

### Tiered SaaS Model

| Tier | Price | Included |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Coordinator Starter** | $49/mo | Up to 15 participants, 3 provider seats |
| **Coordinator Pro** | $99/mo | Unlimited participants, 10 provider seats |
| **Clinic Team** | $249/mo | Unlimited participants, unlimited providers, admin dashboard |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Multiple locations, SLA, dedicated support |

### Provider-Facing
- Free for providers receiving referrals (network effect)
- Optional premium: $19/mo/provider for standalone provider accounts
- Clinic packages: $99-$299/mo depending on therapist count

### Revenue Per Participant (RPM)
- Support Coordinator pays: $3-5/participant/month
- At 30 participants/coordinator = $90-150/coordinator/month

## Go-to-Market Channels

### 1. NDIS Sector Events
- **Priority:** ADHS (Australian Disability Services) Conference, NDS Sector Summit
- **Tactics:** Booth presence, demo station, sponsored workshop
- **Cost:** $5,000-15,000/event
- **Expected ROI:** 5-10 coordinator signups per event

### 2. NDIS Sector Publications
- **Publications:** Disability Support Magazine, The Sector, Inside NDIS newsletter
- **Content:** Case studies, thought leadership on care coordination
- **Cost:** $2,000-5,000/piece
- **Expected:** 2-3 coordinator signups per article

### 3. Peak Body Partnerships
- **Targets:** National Disability Services (NDS), Allied Health Professions Australia (AHPA)
- **Model:** Partnership agreement, referral pathway, co-branded content
- **Cost:** Time investment, potential co-marketing budget
- **Expected:** Credibility + warm referrals

### 4. Referral Program
- **Mechanism:** Existing coordinators refer new coordinators
- **Incentive:** 1 month free for referrer + referred
- **Expected:** 20% of new signups through referral

### 5. Direct Sales (Founder-Led)
- **Target:** 20 largest Support Coordination practices in Australia
- **Method:** Cold outreach + warm intro via sector contacts
- **Cycle:** 6-8 weeks from first contact to signed contract
- **Focus:** Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane first (highest density)

### 6. Product-Led Growth (Later Phase)
- **Free tier** for individual providers
- **In-app prompts** for coordinators when they invite providers
- **Viral mechanics:** Provider invites provider through referral link
- **Target:** 50 registered providers organically within 6 months

## Launch Sequence

### Private Beta (Months 1-3)
**Goal:** 5 Support Coordinators, 50 active participants

- Hand-picked coordinators with existing provider networks
- White-glove onboarding (founder personally helps setup)
- Feedback loops: weekly calls, Slack channel
- Key metric: NPS > 50, weekly active usage > 80%

### Public Beta (Months 4-6)
**Goal:** 20 Support Coordinators, 300 active participants

- Open signup for coordinators
- Referral program launched
- First 2 NDIS sector event appearances
- First case study published (with permission)
- Integration with 1 plan management software

### Official Launch (Month 7-9)
**Goal:** 50 Coordinators, 1,000 participants

- Press release + sector media outreach
- Launch webinar with 3 beta customers presenting
- NDIS Commission engagement (awareness, not endorsement)
- Partnership discussions with allied health groups
- Pricing page live with free trial

### Scale Phase (Months 10-18)
**Goal:** 150 Coordinators, 5,000 participants

- Outbound sales team (2 SDRs + 1 Account Exec)
- Integration partnerships (Practice Management software)
- Enterprise tier launched
- Adelaide and Perth market expansion
- Operational automation (less white-glove onboarding)

## Competitive Positioning

### Direct Competitors
- **Coordinator Pro / Support Works:** Existing practice management tools, not participant-centric
- **Therapy Notes / Halaxy:** Provider-centric practice management, no referral/referral flow

### Indirect Competitors
- **Email + PDFs + phone:** Current default (what we're replacing)
- **NDIS myplace portal:** Participant records but no provider collaboration

### Our Moat
1. **Participant control:** Data belongs to participant, not providers
2. **Consent-first:** Every data share is explicit, auditable, revocable
3. **Structured data:** Not PDFs — machine-readable, queryable, reportable
4. **Network effect:** More coordinators = more providers = more participants
5. **NDIS Commission ready:** Audit trail built in from day one

### Positioning Statement
> "Care Exchange is the participant-controlled coordination layer for the NDIS — connecting the care team through secure referrals, structured updates, and shared goal tracking, so participants don't have to repeat their story every time they change providers."

## Retention & Expansion

### Churn Prevention
- **Health score:** Monitored per coordinator (update frequency, participant engagement)
- **Monthly review:** Touch point at month 1, 3, 6, 12
- **At-risk flags:** No logins for 7 days, update rate dropping
- **Success metrics:** Participants per coordinator, updates per participant per month

### Expansion Revenue
- **Coordinator → Clinic tier:** When they add more coordinators
- **Participant growth:** More participants = higher tier
- **Provider upsell:** Providers pay for premium features

### Customer Success
- **Onboarding:** Dedicated 30-min setup call + Slack channel
- **Training:** Monthly webinar for new users
- **Content:** Video tutorials, help centre, template library
- **Support:** Email + in-app chat during business hours
