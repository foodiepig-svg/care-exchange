# Care Exchange — User Personas

---

## 1. Priya Sharma — The NDIS Participant

### Demographics
- **Age:** 31
- **Location:** Melbourne, VIC
- **Condition:** Cerebral palsy (mobility support needs)
- **Plan:** NDIS Plan 3 (self-managed)
- **Occupation:** UX Designer (remote contractor)

### Goals & Motivations
- Maintain control over who sees her personal information and care plans
- Reduce the effort of coordinating between multiple providers
- Track progress toward personal goals without relying on her Support Coordinator
- Share information with new providers quickly when she changes services

### Pain Points
- "Every time I switch physios, I have to re-explain my whole history"
- "I have 5 different apps for 5 different providers — none of them talk to each other"
- "I never know what my Support Coordinator actually does all month"
- "My plan manager handles the money, but nobody helps me coordinate the actual care"
- "Providers send me PDFs of reports I'll never read again"

### How Care Exchange Helps
- "One secure link and my new physio has everything they need"
- "I decide exactly what each provider can see — and I can revoke it"
- "Instead of long reports, providers send me 3-bullet updates I actually understand"
- "I can see all my goals in one place and track them myself"

### User Story
> "I switched from one occupational therapy provider to another. Normally that takes weeks of emails and form-filling. With Care Exchange, I sent a referral link, the new OT accepted it, and within 24 hours they had my goals, current equipment, and a progress note from my last session. The whole thing took me 5 minutes."

### Key Metrics
- Time to onboard new provider: 24 hours (vs 2-3 weeks traditional)
- Care team visibility: 100% of active providers in one view
- Goals reviewed monthly: 3 of 3 (vs 1 of 5 previously)

## 2. David Chen — The Support Coordinator

### Demographics
- **Age:** 38
- **Role:** Support Coordinator at Mosaic Support Services
- **Caseload:** 22 participants
- **Location:** Brisbane, QLD
- **Background:** Social Work, 8 years disability sector

### Goals & Motivations
- Reduce time spent on administrative coordination (currently 60% of week)
- Maintain compliance and audit trails for NDIS Commission
- Keep participants and families informed without constant manual updates
- Identify when participants are falling through gaps before crises occur

### Pain Points
- "I spend half my week chasing providers for progress updates"
- "Participants don't know what's happening between visits"
- "If I take leave, nobody knows what's going on with my caseload"
- "When a participant changes providers, I start from scratch every time"
- "Compliance paperwork takes longer than actual support"

### How Care Exchange Helps
- "Progress updates come in structured — I read them in 2 minutes instead of 20"
- "Participants see the same information I do, automatically"
- "My caseload overview shows me who hasn't had an update in 4+ weeks"
- "Every referral I send is already documented and audit-ready"

### User Story
> "I had a participant whose psychology sessions stopped. No one told me — the family assumed I knew. My caseload dashboard flagged it after 6 weeks of no updates. I contacted the psychology practice, discovered a scheduling miscommunication, and got sessions rebooked. Before this platform, that gap would have lasted months."

### Key Metrics
- Time spent on administration per week: down from 60% to 35%
- Participants with active updates in last 30 days: 21 of 22 (vs 12 of 22)
- Time to resolve care gaps: 3 days average (vs 6 weeks)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 44

- **Organisation:** 35 therapists across physio, OT, speech pathology
- **Location:** Sydney, NSW
- **NDIS Registered:** Yes

### Goals & Motivations
- Reduce intake time when receiving new participant referrals
- Ensure therapists document in ways that meet NDIS Commission requirements

### Pain Points
- "Getting a new referral means phone calls, emails, waiting for files"
- "Therapists write notes in completely different formats"

- "We've lost participants because the intake process felt slow and bureaucratic"

### How Care Exchange Helps
- "New referral link arrives with participant background already summarised"
- "Structured updates mean all therapists write the same format — plan reviews are easier"
- "I can see all our active participants' update status in one dashboard"
- "We've reduced intake from 2 weeks to 3 days"

### User Story
> "We received a complex referral — pediatric OT for a child with autism. The referral link included the child's goals from his plan, notes from his speech pathologist, and his NDIS plan number with allocated hours. Our OT was fully prepared for the first session. The mother commented that she'd never had a provider be so prepared."

### Key Metrics
- Average intake time: 3 days (vs 14 days industry average)
- Plan review prep time: 45 minutes (vs 4 hours)
- Participants retained after 12 months: 89% (up from 71%)

- Provider information consistency: 100% (vs ~60%)

## 5. Sarah Wellington — The Allied Health Therapist

### Demographics
- **Age:** 29
- **Role:** Physiotherapist, Apex Allied Health
- **Experience:** 4 years, specialises in neurological rehabilitation
- **Location:** Sydney, NSW

### Goals & Motivations
- Spend more time with clients and less time writing notes
- Communicate participant progress in ways participants and families understand
- Build a track record of measurable outcomes for plan reviews
- Collaborate easily with other therapists on shared participants

### Pain Points
- "Progress notes take me 20 minutes per session — I do 6 sessions a day"
- "I write for other therapists, not for the participant"
- "I never know what the other providers are doing unless I email them"
- "NDIS plan reviews require data I don't have time to compile"

### How Care Exchange Helps
- "Structured updates take 5 minutes — I pick a category, write 3 bullets"
- "Updates are written for the participant first, family second"
- "I can see what the OT is working on without a separate meeting"
- "Goals have progress tracking — I log movement metrics each session"

### User Story
> "I had a participant post-stroke. Between sessions, I logged that his standing tolerance improved from 2 minutes to 8 minutes over 6 weeks. At the plan review, I pulled up his Care Exchange goal timeline — the NDIS planner was impressed by the measurable progress data. His plan was continued without question."

### Key Metrics
- Time writing notes per session: 5 minutes (vs 20 minutes)
- Participant goal progress documented: 100% of sessions
- Cross-provider communication incidents: 0 per month (vs 3-4)

## 6. James Blackwood — The NDIS Plan Manager

### Demographics
- **Age:** 47
- **Role:** Director, Blackwood Plan Management
- **Organisation:** Plan management for 180 participants
- **Location:** Adelaide, SA

### Goals & Motivations
- Verify that services claimed against plans were actually delivered
- Reduce friction between providers and participants around funding
- Maintain clean audit trails for NDIS Commission compliance
- Identify participants whose plans are underspent with risk of rollover loss

### Pain Points
- "Providers send invoices in PDF, email, sometimes fax — no standard format"
- "I have to manually match every invoice to a service booking"
- "Participants don't know what's been claimed until they get their statement"
- "Plan underspend is a huge issue — participants lose funding at plan end"

### How Care Exchange Helps
- "Referrals and service delivery are linked — funding follows the care relationship"
- "Providers submit claims with structured service records attached"
- "Participants see real-time what has been delivered against their plan"
- "I can flag underspend 8 weeks before plan end — not 2 weeks"

### User Story
> "I noticed a participant had $15,000 remaining in her Core budget with 6 weeks left in her plan. Normally I'd never know until the statement. Care Exchange flagged the underspend — I contacted her Support Coordinator, found she needed more OT sessions, and arranged six sessions before her plan ended. Her funding was used effectively, not lost."

### Key Metrics
- Invoice processing time: 2 minutes per invoice (vs 15 minutes)
- Plan underspend identified early: 89% of cases (vs 23%)
- Audit trail completeness: 100% digital (vs mixed paper/digital)
